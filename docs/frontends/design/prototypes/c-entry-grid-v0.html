Guided tour — the hero scenario (FY2028 OPEX, Sales division)
  1. Start editing: the view opens read-only — click ✏️ Start editing in the banner to check the slice out (editing is an explicit act; a taken slice shows who holds it and offers the v1 admin force-release).
  2. Top-down entry: click the Sales Division × FY cell (top-right), type 120M, Enter → the target spreads proportionally to every unlocked leaf (light blue).
  3. Override + lock: click Marketing · Campaigns × Mar, type +2M, Enter, then press L to lock it (amber 🔒).
  4. Re-spread around the lock: click Sales Division × FY again, type 120M → total reconciles to exactly 120 000; the locked campaign cell is untouched.
  5. Locked total + block op: click Field Sales × FY, press L. Drag-select the Field Sales leaf cells Jan–Jun, type -10 in the % box, Apply → H2 months catch up (violet) because the row total must hold.
  6. Over-constrained = error: also lock Field Sales × Q3 and × Q4, repeat step 4 → the operation is rejected and rolled back.
  7. Zero base = error, seed explicitly: Marketing · Software is empty (not budgeted). Enter a value on its FY cell → zero-base error. Select the row's months, click Seed from ACT2027 (green), then enter the total again.
  8. Seasonality: select 12 months of one row, pick a curve, Apply → the block re-profiles, each row's block total preserved.
  9. The reservation (check-out/check-in): the green banner shows you hold the Sales Division slice · TTL is informational only in v1 (stale reservations are broken by an admin — the banner shows whom to call). Click 😈 Petra tries to check out Field Sales → denied: it overlaps your reservation. Cube data doesn't merge, so conflicts are prevented up front, not detected after.
  10. Preview the check-in: click Preview & check in → in the journal panel → the dry run shows each assignment's expansion to storage grain (the two cost centers under every department cell — proportional over the existing base, lock-free). Red-dotted cells (Marketing·Software) have no storage base: an entry there previews as a zero-base error until its seed op is in the batch.
  11. Check in: commit the batch → entry record card (principal, program, run, leaf-set hash) → choose keep working (reservation held, view reloads exactly — nothing else could have touched your slice) or release (read-only; try Petra's button again — now she gets the slice).

The two-pass model (E, amended 2026-07-10): everything on this screen is the visible pass — an in-memory working view at the form's loaded scope (collapse hides cells, never unloads them); every mechanism here is non-authoritative tooling, and locks & formulas are FE-only, ephemeral, never stored. On check-in (requires your held reservation — check-out/check-in, SVN-style: cube data doesn't merge), only the ratified assignments (journal →) go to the entry journal; the canon opex-entry-apply TTR-P program re-derives cubelet leaves with the single, lock-free committed mechanism: per assignment, proportional over the existing base beneath it — zero base ⇒ error, largest-remainder rounding. Under the reservation the base cannot drift; the drift check survives as an internal invariant.
