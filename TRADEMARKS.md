# Trademark Policy

"Tatrman" and "Tatrman Server" are trademarks of **Collite** (Collite s.r.o.). The
software is open source under Apache-2.0; the **name** is not — the mark is how a
user can trust that something called "Tatrman" is the real thing. This policy
explains what you may and may not do with the name. It is written to be valid
before formal registration completes (an EU trademark application is planned).

The Apache-2.0 license grants you broad rights over the **code**. It does **not**
grant rights to the **trademarks** (Apache-2.0 §6 is explicit about this). This
policy is the separate, deliberately permissive grant for ordinary, truthful use.

## What you may do without asking

- **Redistribute unmodified builds** of Tatrman under the name "Tatrman".
- **State compatibility truthfully**: "works with Tatrman", "compatible with
  Tatrman", "built on Tatrman", "for Tatrman". Nominative use to refer to the
  project is always fine.
- **Use the name in discussion, teaching, articles, and talks.**

## What you may not do without permission

- **Distribute a modified fork under the name "Tatrman"** (or a confusingly
  similar name). Fork freely — the license lets you — but give your fork its own
  name so users are not misled about what they are running.
- **Imply endorsement or certification** you do not have — e.g. "certified",
  "official", or "Tatrman-conformant" (see below) when it is not true.
- **Use the marks in your company name, product name, or logo** in a way that
  suggests Collite is the source of your product.

## Conformance is how the name is earned

The forward-looking use of the mark is **conformance**. An independent
implementation that passes the core **conformance conversation suite** will be
entitled to describe itself as **"Tatrman-conformant"**. This is the whole point
of the standard: third parties can self-verify and then say so, truthfully.

The conformance program is **coming with 1.0** — the suite is being authored and
no conformance claim is available yet. Until it exists, no one (including us) is
"Tatrman-conformant".

The marks are owned by Collite. For any use not clearly covered above — or if you
are unsure — ask first: `trademarks@tatrman.org`. We intend to be permissive with
good-faith, non-confusing use.
